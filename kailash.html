The Call

Kailash does not receive visitors.
It receives those it has called.

For thousands of years, seekers have crossed deserts and rivers to reach this mountain — not to conquer it, for no one climbs Kailash, but to walk around it. To circumambulate the axis of the world. To bow before what will never need to be understood.

This June, Mohanji leads a small gathering of prepared souls on the fifty-two kilometre parikrama — three days of walking at altitude, in silence and in prayer, under the unblinking eye of the mountain.

— Yatra bheda nasti · Where distinction ends —
Four Sacred Pillars

What this pilgrimage will be

Not a tour. Not an adventure. A deliberate, guided immersion in the four most sacred thresholds of the Kailash region.

I.

The Parikrama

Three days walking 52 km around the mountain — Darchen to Dirapuk, over the 5,645 m Drolma La pass, down to Zuthulpuk, and back. Each step a mantra. Each breath an offering.

II.

Lake Manasarovar

Dawn at 4,590 m beside the highest freshwater lake on earth — born, the Puranas say, from the mind of Brahma. Holy bath, fire ceremony, and Mohanji's satsang at the water's edge.

III.

Ashtapad Darshan

The rarely-visited southern face — believed by Jains to be the liberation seat of Rishabhanatha. Private darshan time in a site almost no yatra offers.

IV.

Transmission with Mohanji

Morning meditation. Evening satsang. Private shaktipat ceremony on the final night at Manasarovar. The inner work that turns a trek into a yatra.

Practical Questions

What you need to know

How fit do I need to be?
You need to be comfortable walking 6–8 hours at altitude over rough terrain. We recommend at least three months of training — hill walking, stair work, and cardio three times a week. A medical fitness certificate is mandatory. Ponies are available on parikrama days for those who need them.
What about altitude sickness?
Real, manageable, and treatable. We build in two full acclimatisation days, carry oxygen on every vehicle, travel with a doctor, and pace the itinerary gradually. Around 5% of yatris find they cannot cross the Drolma La and return early — we plan for this with dignity and care.
Will Mohanji really be with us the whole time?
Yes. From the opening satsang in Kathmandu on Day 1 to the closing circle on Day 14, Mohanji is with the group throughout. He walks the parikrama at the pace of the slowest yatri. He does not rush. He does not leave anyone behind.
Can I come alone?
Over half of our yatris travel solo. You will arrive as a stranger and leave as something closer than family. We pair solo yatris thoughtfully for twin-share accommodation, or you can upgrade to the Yogi tier for single occupancy.
What's the group size?
Capped at 48 yatris total across all tiers. Pilgrim satsangs are capped at 20. Yogi satsangs at 8. The cap protects the quality of the inner experience — not a marketing number.
What happens if the route closes?
The Tibetan Autonomous Region occasionally restricts access on short notice. If the yatra cannot run, registered yatris are offered either a full refund or transfer to the 2027 group. We hold insurance against political closure at the operator level.

About Mohanji

A life poured

The Journey

From a quiet boy in Kerala
to a global presence.

The outline of a life that has moved, almost without pause, from the inner landscape of silence to the outer work of serving humanity.

Mohanji was born in a small village in Kerala, South India — a child known for his unusual stillness and for a natural affinity with the world around him. From an early age he was drawn inward, to the interior rooms where questions of purpose, being and suffering live. The outer life, though, unfolded in the ordinary way: school, university, a career in the shipping industry that would eventually carry him across oceans and into leadership roles in Dubai, Oman, Nigeria and beyond.

Through those years the inner pull never left him. He served quietly — helping where he could, giving without announcement — and walked alongside saints and masters of India's living traditions. Loss, love, and a long apprenticeship to silence shaped the teacher he would later become. When the call to teach publicly arrived, he did not rush to meet it. It arrived, in its own time, in 2007 — and by 2012 Mohanji had begun to speak openly to a growing circle of sincere seekers.

What he offered was not new. It was as old as the Himalayas: the recognition that we are consciousness, that ahimsa — non-violence — is the highest practice, and that the shortest path to the divine is to be, simply and unapologetically, oneself. Today that offering has grown into Mohanji Foundation, a network of humanitarian organisations, and a family of practitioners across more than ninety countries — held together not by hierarchy, but by a shared intention to be kind, to be useful, and to be free.

The Teaching

Three threads,
one simple life.

Mohanji's teaching is not a doctrine. It is a set of doorways back to what you already are.

I.

Be You.

Do not imitate. Do not perform. The divine is not elsewhere — it is the unconditioned version of who you already are. The entire path is the steady uncovering of that self, and the gentle shedding of everything you are not.

II.

Ahimsa in action.

Non-violence is not passivity. It is the active choice — in thought, word, food and work — to do no harm and, wherever possible, to bring relief. It begins on the plate and extends outward to every being we touch.

III.

Service as sadhana.

Selfless service is not charity done on the side — it is the practice itself. To serve without expectation dissolves the ego faster than any meditation seat, and turns ordinary life into a continuous offering.

A Life in Motion

Markers along the way.

Kerala

Early years

Born in a village in South India. A childhood marked by inwardness, long hours in nature, and a tenderness toward all living beings that his family noticed from the beginning.

2000s

A working life, quietly spiritual

Years of leadership in the international shipping industry — Dubai, the Middle East, Africa — while walking, in parallel, with saints and masters of India's living traditions. Service offered without name.

Ammucare begins

Founded Ammucare Charitable Trust in memory of his daughter Ammu — an act of grief transmuted into ongoing service: food, medicine, education and disaster relief for those who have the least.

2012

Teaching publicly

Begins teaching openly. The first Mohanji Foundation circles form. A practice rooted in ahimsa, selfless service and the direct recognition of one's own nature begins to spread quietly across continents.

Today

A global family

Nineteen Mohanji Spaces across six continents. Family in more than ninety countries. Ongoing humanitarian work through Ammucare, ACT Foundation and allied initiatives — and a weekly talk, open to all, that has never stopped.

Not a guru
in the old shape.

Mohanji consistently declines the architecture of guru and disciple. He does not collect followers. He does not sell liberation. "I am what you consider me to be," he has said — meaning the relationship is yours to define, and the work is yours to do.

What he offers instead is companionship on the path: a steady, patient presence pointing always back to your own centre. The Mohanji family is therefore horizontal rather than vertical — a fellowship of seekers supporting one another, with Mohanji as the quiet reference point rather than the destination.

This is, perhaps, the most contemporary thing about a very ancient teaching: it insists on your sovereignty. You are not asked to surrender your discrimination, only to use it well.

The Work in the World

Organisations of benevolence.

Over two decades a constellation of charities, schools and platforms has grown around Mohanji's work — each with its own focus, each offering its service freely.

M

Mohanji Foundation

Headquarters · Switzerland

The central body coordinating teaching, retreats, publications and the worldwide network of Mohanji Spaces — present in more than fifteen registered countries.

A

Ammucare Charitable Trust

Humanitarian · Founded 2007

Food, medicine, education, disaster relief. Ammucare carries Mohanji's principle that selfless service is not a wing of the practice — it is the practice.

A

ACT Foundation

Awareness · Compassion · Transformation

A global humanitarian arm active across India, the Balkans, Africa and the Americas — mobilising volunteers in moments of crisis and in quiet, daily care.

H

Himalayan School of Traditional Yoga

Lineage · Practice · Transmission

A school carrying Himalayan yogic traditions — asana, pranayama, meditation and the deeper internal practices — transmitted in their classical, unhurried form.

W

World Consciousness Alliance

Gathering · Dialogue · Awareness

A platform bringing together teachers, healers and seekers from across traditions to serve the rise of collective consciousness beyond any single lineage.

E

Early Birds Club

Daily practice · Worldwide

A simple, powerful daily practice shared across timezones — an early-morning container that millions of practitioners return to, one quiet sunrise at a time.
